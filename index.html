Goal of this event

  1. The objective of this Summit is essentially to further instill an entrepreneurial and futuristic mindset amongst the African youth community in Malaysia.
  2. The Summit is also focused on engaging with the said youth community through highlighting the significanceof Social Finance and identifying investment opportunities that are necessary for the advancement of their respective communities
  3. In addition, it aims to give the respective participants a better understanding of the working world (and financial market). These would be achieved through:
      Panel discussions
      Workshops
      Pitch Competition

Scopes of this event

  1. Addressing the Elephant in the Continent: Elephant – Why isn’t/hasn't Africa Advanced Economically (female representation)!
      Identifying and highlighting key problems (in relation to the lack of Social Finance)
      Opportunities underutilized (infrastructure, farming, etc.)
      Efforts that have been made towards the betterment of the State
  1. Looking Beyond the African Horizon: Giraffe – What is Needed for a Better Tomorrow
      Final Destination – What is the future/what lies ahead
      Driving Factors Necessary for the Development:
        Technology integration (Smart Contract, Fintech, etc,)
        Regulatory framework/policy (copyright, protection of intellectual property)
        Financing (social finance through online crowdfunding platforms and others)
        Research & talent training programs
      Key stakeholders and their role:
        Government
        Private sector
        Third sector
        Family Unit
        Individuals
      Learning from success stories (insight from non-African: Malaysian rep.)
  1. Confronting a Predator: Blue Morpho Butterfly – How to Pitch to investors (25th Oct.)
      Feasibility and viability of startup/idea
      Business model
      What's in it for investors
      What not to do
  2. Swimming in Deep Waters: Octopus– Developing an Entrepreneurial Backbone and Surviving the Market
      Should one even become an entrepreneur?
      What it takes to become an entrepreneur
      Starter-kits
  3. How to Catch a Prey: Jaguar – What to Focus on NOW
      Where to start (developing CV, networking, etc.)
      Developing crucial skillsets today (basic Microsoft, assertive communication, etc.)
  1. Social Finance and its role in developing African communities: crowdfunding, waqf, takaful, etc.
  2. Technological advances and their contribution to social welfare: Crypto Assets= Blockchain, Smart Contract, etc.
  3. Identifying investment opportunities and side income for lower income group (B40)
